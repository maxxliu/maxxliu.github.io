Is Technology Backward Looking?

Technology is Forward Looking

To give more context, given my understanding of the lecture, the professor defines forward-looking as having a picture of where you want to be in the future and developing strategies to get there. An example given in class is that of a game theory model with 3 actors. At each stage, one of the actors can make one of two decisions that can impact their final payoff. Given the entire decision tree, the actors can then look forward and work backwards to see which decisions will give them the most optimal payoff. Technology is backward-looking because technologists look back and see all that they/society had accomplished, and then decide from there where to go/build next. I disagree with this view on how or why people build new technology.

After seeing so many legendary technology visionaries rise over the last few decades: Steve Jobs, Elon Musk, Bill Gates, Ed Catmull, Jeff Bezos, etc., it is hard to imagine that these people developed technology simply by looking back and deciding that their product was the logical next step. Each technologist has a vision for what they want the future to look like regardless of whether or not past technologies are pointing in that direction. In fact, the opposite happens, entrepreneurs are constantly envisioning a better future and creating new technologies to get them there, often leaving behind and making obsolete the old technology.

If being forward-looking means taking concrete steps and making decisions to achieve a vision for the future (or an expected payoff in the economic sense) then technology must be forward-looking.
